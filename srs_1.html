Section 1: Intro

Most SQL users intuitively understand the results of an INNER JOIN, a LEFT OUTER JOIN, and a FULL OUTER JOIN, especially when joining on just one field. However, understanding the result sets when joining on multiple fields or when not using Equi Joins becomes more complicated. I believe it is useful to understand logically why an RDBMS returns the results that it does. It is important to note that this post is NOT an attempt to explain the execution plan of the RDBMS, but rather to explain logically why result sets are returned.
		
In this post, I will explain the following concepts through examples: 

1) The results of any INNER JOIN operation can be understood by examining the Cartesian product

2) The results of any LEFT OUTER JOIN operation can be derived by the following:

Results of INNER JOIN operation		
UNION		
Records in Left table NOT in INNER JOIN results 

3) The results of any RIGHT OUTER JOIN operation can be derived by the following:

Results of INNER JOIN operation		
UNION		
Records in Right table NOT in INNER JOIN results 		

4) The results of any FULL OUTER JOIN operation can be derived by the following:

Results of INNER JOIN operation		
UNION
Records in Left table NOT in INNER JOIN results 
UNION
Records in Right table NOT in INNER JOIN results
